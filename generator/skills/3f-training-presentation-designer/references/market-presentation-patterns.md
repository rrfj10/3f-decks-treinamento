# Market Presentation Patterns

Use these references as design principles, not as templates to copy directly.

## Validated Reference Set

- `baoyu-slide-deck` on skills.sh: useful staged flow for outline, review, generation, and iteration.
- `slidev` on skills.sh: useful reference for web-native presentations, presenter flow, exports, and structured content.
- `frontend-slides` on GitHub: useful reminder that HTML decks need visual polish, responsiveness, and frontend validation.
- `presentation-master` on skills.sh: useful synthesis of Duarte, Garr Reynolds, Guy Kawasaki, TED-style delivery, and Tufte-inspired clarity.
- Presentation Zen / Garr Reynolds: reduce clutter, use restraint, make slides support the speaker instead of replacing the speaker.
- Nancy Duarte: structure a presentation around audience transformation, tension, contrast, and a clear destination.
- Edward Tufte: preserve data integrity, avoid decorative noise in charts and operational information.
- Guy Kawasaki: keep business presentations concise and time-aware.
- TED-style talks: make the core idea memorable, concrete, and story-driven.

## Apply to 3F Training

- Start with the operational problem and why it matters.
- Define what the learner should do differently after the training.
- Use examples from the operation whenever possible.
- Convert abstract guidance into behaviors, scripts, checklists, or decision rules.
- Include practice when the topic involves judgment, atendimento, processo, comportamento, qualidade, compliance, lideranca, vendas, retencao, cobranca, planejamento, NOC, MIS, SLA, escala, forecast, ou KPIs.
- Include assessment when the training needs evidence of understanding or certification.
- Prefer slide titles that state the point, not only the subject.

## Slide Architecture

Use this default arc unless the user gives a stronger structure:

1. Capa
2. Contexto
3. Objetivo
4. Por que importa
5. Conceitos principais
6. Processo ou metodo
7. Exemplo pratico
8. Boas praticas
9. Erros comuns
10. Atividade
11. Avaliacao ou checagem
12. Resumo e proximos passos

Adjust slide count to duration:

- 10 to 15 minutes: 5 to 8 slides.
- 20 to 30 minutes: 8 to 14 slides.
- 45 to 60 minutes: 14 to 22 slides.
- More than 60 minutes: split into modules.

## Quality Checks

- Every slide must answer: what should the learner understand or do?
- No slide should rely on a long paragraph as the main content.
- Repeated concepts should be merged unless repetition serves practice or reinforcement.
- If the topic is operational, include a scenario or decision example.
- If the topic is behavioral, include observable behaviors.
- If the topic is process-oriented, include the flow, checkpoints, and exceptions.
